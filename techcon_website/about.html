History

Arm's flagship developer event began in 2004 as DevCon, a modest gathering at the Santa Clara Convention Center dedicated to uniting processor experts, software developers, and hardware architects around ARM-based system innovation. By 2008, the conference had rebranded to Arm TechCon, evolving into a three-day flagship event highlighting embedded design, IoT proliferation, cloud-edge integration, and cutting-edge compute topics like AI and 5G. It served as the platform for major ARM milestones—keynotes such as Simon Segars “Fifth Wave” vision, the introduction of Total Compute architecture, and initiatives like Project Cassini and SystemReady. Following the disruptions of 2020-2022, TechCon transformed into Arm DevSummit, adopting an online format and exploring a broader developer ecosystem, from software tools to security layers.

Running in parallel yet distinct, the SRC TECHCON, organized by the Semiconductor Research Corporation since at least 1990, is an annual hub in Austin where academia and industry converge to showcase semiconductor research breakthroughs. From early work on graphene nanoribbons and TCAD simulation tools to recent student-led poster sessions and technical awards, SRC TECHCON has nurtured university-driven innovation and engineer recruitment. The 2024 edition featured over 200 research scholars, with nearly half women or under-represented minorities, and emphasized collaboration among top semiconductor firms, academic institutions, and future engineers

Mission

TechCon 2024 aims to be the premier technology conference, bringing together innovators, developers, and industry leaders from around the world. The event will showcase cutting-edge advancements, inspiring talks, and hands-on sessions covering a wide range of tech domains—from AI and cybersecurity to web development and emerging technologies. Whether you're a seasoned professional or an aspiring tech enthusiast, TechCon 2024 offers valuable insights, networking opportunities, and a glimpse into the future of technology.
